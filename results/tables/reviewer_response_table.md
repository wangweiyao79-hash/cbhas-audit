**This reverses the single-seed impression.** At seed=42 alone, CB-HAS+FL(fixed) looked 6.26 pp worse than CB-HAS+CE (Section 4.4, Finding 3) — but across 5 seeds the gap is -0.26 pp with a CI comfortably straddling zero (p=0.75): CB-HAS+CE and CB-HAS+FL(fixed) are statistically indistinguishable. ## M5 — Sampling-budget fairness (matched)

**SUPERSEDED (2026-07-11): the original M5 result below was contaminated and is retracted.** `resample()`'s memory-safety cap silently downsampled BENIGN and three other classes to 50,000 samples each (from 1,257,033 / 103,708 / 76,808 / 54,416) before ROS/SMOTE/ADASYN ran — a step CB-HAS never undergoes. This was never a budget-matching mechanism; it deleted ~87% of the majority-class training data for these three baselines only. Fixed in `train.py` (`resample()` now skips the cap entirely on the matched-budget path, with a runtime self-check asserting every non-augmented class is byte-for-byte preserved).
